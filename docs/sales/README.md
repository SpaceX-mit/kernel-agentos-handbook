# docs/sales — The Sales Motion

> Operational documents for taking an open-source substrate from
> "published" to "paying customers." Four artifacts that together
> form the complete week-1 through month-12 sales motion. All
> CC BY 4.0 — forkable into your own context.

These are tools, not marketing copy. Each doc is meant to be
copy-pasted, edited, and used. The activation cost from "I have
the doc" to "I sent the first DM / signed the first pilot / pitched
the first investor" is intentionally minutes, not days.

## The four docs

### 1. [`revenue-paths.md`](./revenue-paths.md) — the map

Ten realistic paths to making money on an open-source substrate,
ranked by time-to-first-dollar. Honest ranges, activation costs,
tradeoffs. Plus a recommended 12-month sequencing for the full-time
builder, the single-decision blocker most builders ignore (day job
vs commitment), and templates for the highest-leverage outbound
moves.

**Read this first** if you're not sure which path to commit to.

### 2. [`buyer-targets.md`](./buyer-targets.md) — the who

Five distinct buyer profiles for kbot-finance-shape substrate
products. Concrete archetypes, channel guidance (LinkedIn / X /
email / warm intro), five outreach templates ready to paste, and
a tracker schema for the data that compounds.

**Read this when** you've picked your path and need a list of
people to contact this week.

### 3. [`validation-calls.md`](./validation-calls.md) — the conversation

The week-1 validation-call script. Five 30-minute calls in seven
days. Five questions with branching logic. The hard-gate decision:
**if ≥3/5 buyers say byte-identical replay is must-have, keep
building the audit-grade architecture; if <2/5, pivot to
log-and-attest only and cut six months of work.**

**Read this when** you've sent the outreach and the first call is
scheduled.

### 4. [`design-partner-sow.md`](./design-partner-sow.md) — the contract

One-page Statement of Work template for the moment a validation
call ends with "yes, interested in a pilot." Two versions
(engineering-led buyer / compliance-led buyer). Pricing, scope,
deliverables, case-study rights, 60-day exclusive subscription
option. Cover email template for the moment of sending.

**Read this when** a buyer has said yes to a pilot and you have
24 hours to send them a SOW.

### 5. [`seed-teaser.md`](./seed-teaser.md) — the deck

Six slides of content (plus an optional moat-slide appendix) for
the moment an investor catches the conversation. Title → Problem →
Insight → Product → Window → Traction + Team → Ask. Pre-written
answers to the three questions every investor asks. Pre-meeting
cover email.

**Read this when** an investor reaches out OR when a third paid
pilot makes a seed round realistic.

## The shape of the year

```
Month 1:    revenue-paths.md → pick the sequencing
            buyer-targets.md → identify 5-10 prospects
            validation-calls.md → run the 5 calls
            
Month 2-3:  validation-calls.md → tally the scoreboard, decide
            design-partner-sow.md → send to the 2-3 strongest yeses
            buyer-targets.md → second wave of 10 prospects
            
Month 4-6:  First pilot delivering
            design-partner-sow.md → second pilot signed
            revenue-paths.md → consulting in parallel for runway
            
Month 7-9:  Three pilots, first case study published
            seed-teaser.md → first investor conversations
            
Month 10-12: Seed round in motion OR doubling down on bootstrapping
             revenue-paths.md → reassess sequencing for year 2
```

## A note on tone

These docs are written in a direct, evidence-cited register. No
"we're excited to" framings. No marketing hype. No fabricated
metrics. The shape is intentionally closer to a memo than a
brochure — readers respect documents that respect their time.

If you fork these into your own context, keep the tone or change it
deliberately. Don't drift accidentally into marketing voice; it
undermines the credibility the rest of the substrate has earned.

## What to do this week

If you haven't started yet, the order is:

1. Read `revenue-paths.md` end-to-end. **30 minutes.**
2. Write your own answer to the "single decision" section
   (commit vs. day job + side build). **15 minutes.**
3. Pick 2-3 paths to commit to for the next 90 days. **15 minutes.**
4. Open `buyer-targets.md` and list 5 specific names. **30 minutes.**
5. Send the first DM using one of the outreach templates. **15 minutes.**

Total: 90 minutes from "I have these docs" to "first outbound sent."
That's the entire activation cost.
